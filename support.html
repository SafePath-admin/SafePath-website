Responder Support Resources

This page is for responders. No speeches. No judgment. Just options.

If you need someone right now

  • Anonymous Responder Chat (SafePath)
    Open the anonymous chat

If you’re in immediate danger, or you can’t stay safe where you are: call your local emergency number, or go to the nearest emergency department.

When you’re not in immediate danger — but you’re not okay

Pick one small next step. You don’t have to solve everything tonight.

  • Text / call a trusted person. One person. One message.
  • Get out of isolation. Sit where someone else is, even quietly.
  • Reduce access to lethal means. Create distance. Add time. Hand it off if you can.
  • Hydrate + eat something simple. Then reassess.
  • Try a 10–15 minute reset. Shower, step outside, slow breathing, or a short walk.

Responder-specific support

If you have access to any of these through your agency or region, use them:

  • Peer Support / CISM / Chaplain
  • Employee Assistance Program (EAP)
  • Union / Benevolent / Association support
  • Supervisor you trust (only if it’s safe to do so)

Confidential crisis lines (U.S.)

These are general crisis resources. Use whatever gets you connected fastest.

  • 988 — Suicide & Crisis Lifeline (call or text)
  • 911 — Emergency

If you’re outside the U.S., use your country’s local emergency number or crisis service.

What SafePath is (and isn’t)

  • SafePath is: a responder-built safety initiative, going live in stages.
  • SafePath isn’t: emergency dispatch, medical care, or a guarantee of confidentiality on your device/network.

If you’re worried about privacy, use a personal device and a network you trust.
